Product Relations

As a Shop Administrator, you can build product relations in your shop base on several product relation types:

  • Related Products
  • Upselling

Location in Administration interface

Product Relations sub-section locates in the Products section on the left on the Administration Interface navigation pane:

From the Product Relations page, Shop Administrators can do the following common and managing actions:

  • Create product relations
  • View product relation in Administration Interface vs view this relation in Shop Application
  • Edit product relations
  • Activate/Deactivate product relations
  • Delete product relation

Managing Product Relations

Creating Product Relations
  1. To create a product relation, in the Administration Interface do the following:
  2. Open Products -> Product Relations section.
  3. On the Product Relations page, click Create Relation on the top.
  4. On the Add Product Relations page, Relation Type tab:

    • Either select the check-box for Update regularly (), or leave the check-box blank.
    • In the Relation type drop-down, select the type (Related products or Upselling) ()
    • In the Selected products section, find an abstract product for which the relation needs to be added and click select in the Actions column. ()

      To easily find the product in the table, you can enter the abstract product SKU into the Search field.

  5. Click Next to proceed to the Assign Products tab. ()
  6. On the Assign products tab, in the Assign related products section:

    • Click Add rule or Add group, depending on the conditions you want to specify. (/)
      • You select Add group () in case you need a set of conditions to be met in order to display the Similar Products section.
      • Add rule () is normally used when there is only one condition that should be met in order to display the Similar Products section for the abstract product.
      • You can specify as many rulles/groups as you need.
      • In case if you added an extra rule/group that is not really needed, you can click Delete next to the specific record to delete it. ()
    • For your rule/group, select the AND or OR operator.

      You use AND operator if you need several conditions to be met, and OR if you need one or another condition to be met.

    • Populate the required drop-downs and fields for your rule/group.
    • Click Save so that your changes are saved and the table automatically updated to display the abstract product(s) that meet the specified rules/groups.

      Your product relation is created, but not active.

  7. To activate the created product relation, click Activate on the top. ()

From this page, you also can:

  1. Click View in the actions column to view the assigned abstract product in Administration Interface. ()
  2. Click View in shop to view the assigned abstract product in Yves. ()
Assign related product rules, groups and types

The rules, as well as the group of rules, are used in order to specify the specific conditions under which this or that similar product should appear on the Abstract Product page, or in the cart.

Let's say you have a Pen as an abstract product, and you need another abstract product Pencil to be displayed as a similar product on the Pen PDP. For this specific case you will specify Related products as a relation type. As a rule, you can select from a wide range of abstract product attributes to specify, like SKU, category, size and so on. You can also specify if the attribute you select is equal, greater, less (or any other value from the list) than a defined value:

Or you can set the Pencil abstract product to appear as a similar product for a Pen in the Cart (once the Pen is actually added to the cart). For that, you will select Upselling as a Relation type and set the appropriate rule.

But if you have a specific requirement to display similar products for Pen only if the SKU of a similar product is equal to some value and the brand is equal to a specific value, you will create a Group of rules:

So the place where the similar product will appear is defined by the relation type value. While the similar product itself is defined by rules, or group of rules.

Viewing a Product Relation

There are 2 places where you can view the product relation:

  • In the Administration Interface
  • In Yves

To view a product relation in the Administration Interface:

  1. In the Product Relations page, click View in the Actions column for a specific product relation. The View Product Relation page opens.
  2. On the View Product Relation page, you can see the following details:
    • General Information: if this relation is in Active state, what is the relation type, what is the SKU and name of the abstract product for which the relation is created. The preview image is also available.
    • Related products: number, SKU, name, price, category and the status of the abstract product(s) that is defined to be a similar one.

To view a product relation in Yves:

  1. On the Product Relations page, click View in Shop in the Actions column for a specific product relation. You are redirected to the abstract product PDP.
  2. On the PDP of an abstract product, scroll down to the Similar products section to see what products are assigned to it as "similars".

When you select the View in Shop option, keep the following in mind:
  • No matter what relation type is, you will be redirected to the abstract PDP page. So if the relation type is up-selling, you will not be able to see Similar products section until you add the product to cart and actually navigate to that cart.
  • The relation you want to view in Yves must be active. If inactive, you will be redirected to the PDP, but there will be no Similar products section for the abstract product. The same applies to upselling.
Editing a Product Relation

To edit a product relation, you need to:

  1. Navigate to Products -> Product Relations.
  2. On the Product relations page, select Edit in the Actions column for the relation you want to edit.
  3. On the Edit Product Relation page, Relation type tab, you can only update the relation type for the abstract product.
  4. On the Assign Products tab, you can update the Assign related products by deleting, adding, or updating the rules and group of rules.
  5. Once done, click Save.
Activating, Deactivating and Deleting Product Relations

To activate, deactivate, or delete a product relation:

  1. Navigate to Products -> Product Relations.
  2. On the Product relations page, find a specific relation.
  3. In the Actions column, select one of the following:
    • Activate , to activate the relation. Once selected the Status is changed to Active.
    • Deactivate , to deactivate the product relation. Once selected the Status is changed to Inactive.
    • Delete , to delete the product relation. Once selected, the relation is deleted from the system.
